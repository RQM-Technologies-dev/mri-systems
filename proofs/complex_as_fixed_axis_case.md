# Proof note: complex MRI as fixed-axis quaternionic case

Given quaternionic state:

\[
q(r)=A(r)\left[\cos\phi(r)+u(r)\sin\phi(r)\right]
\]

Choose fixed axis \(u(r)=i\) for all \(r\). Then:

\[
q(r)=A(r)\left[\cos\phi(r)+i\sin\phi(r)\right]=A(r)e^{i\phi(r)}=z(r)
\]

Therefore standard complex MRI representation is exactly the fixed-axis special
case of the quaternionic formulation.
